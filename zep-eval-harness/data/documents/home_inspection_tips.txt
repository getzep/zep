Home Inspection: What Buyers Need to Know

A home inspection is one of the most important steps in the buying process. A qualified inspector will evaluate the property's structure, systems, and overall condition.

Key Areas Inspected:
- Roof condition and estimated remaining lifespan
- Foundation and structural integrity
- Electrical system (panel, wiring, outlets)
- Plumbing (pipes, water heater, fixtures)
- HVAC system age and condition
- Windows and doors for proper sealing
- Attic insulation and ventilation

Red Flags to Watch For:
- Water damage or stains on ceilings and walls
- Cracks in the foundation wider than 1/4 inch
- Outdated electrical panels (Federal Pacific or Zinsco)
- Evidence of pest damage, especially termites
- Mold or musty odors in basements and crawl spaces

After the Inspection:
You can negotiate repairs with the seller, request a price reduction, or walk away if major issues are found. Most purchase contracts include an inspection contingency giving you 7-10 days to complete the inspection.
